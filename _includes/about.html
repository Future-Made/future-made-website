About the imaginables

This is an open call for all individuals and communities of the world; who are into ideation, design, research, and development activities on observing the civic engagements and the impact of demand-side-initiated or promoted future scenarios (aka. demand-augmentation) and creating opportunities to extend the Freedom of Expression with Expression of Demand in several fields.

Future, Made is an independent Art & Technology collective hoping to be creative on what it does + how and why it exists. Opted for making a positive impact via progressively conducting a set of experimental projects in the socio-economical and political aspects of a multi-lateral, inclusive and globally designable future; while fostering commons-making and skills-making, around new socio-economical value chains with humanitarian values in a grasroots fashion.

Future is observable, when there is a demand, intention and consent bases exist for efficient, effective and fair access to distribution, discovery and use of resources. Otherwise, there will be an ongoing need for historical-data that is being collected by some, in real-time. We should be ready for a future, where all ads are blocked because of the known and being experienced/forced, bad practices. Our technology-enabled projects have also inspired from Community Supported Agriculture, where a future clarity is given to farmers by the consumers.

All concepts and projects are being developed to further increase the accessibility, sustainability of the Arts, Culture and Knowlegde, opening new channels for expressing local and global problems via causes included to improve the ability of people to organize and demand changes in an non-anthropocentric manner, with an initial dedication on fostering green, safe, localized, decentralize-able and deferrable art events of the future, by design.

Hence, efforts started with improving the, deterministic and risk-taking performances ticketing/planning methodologies. and creating future-oriented, temporary and/or long-term networks via creating micro-incentives and multi-lateral beneficiary sharings between participants.
